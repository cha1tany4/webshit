Webshit is a curated archive documenting the technical, ethical, psychological, and systematic failures of modern web environments.

## Why This Exists

The web has a problem. Somewhere between chasing engagement metrics and optimising for ad revenue, we broke something fundamental. Pages that take 10 seconds to load. Consent dialogs that make opting out impossible. Infinite scroll designed to hijack your attention. Newsletters that harvest your email to sell to data brokers.

We document these failures — not to doomscroll about them, but to name them clearly, understand why they happen, and point toward better approaches.

## What We Cover

- **Technical failures** — Bloated JavaScript, slow load times, broken accessibility
- **Ethical failures** — Dark patterns, surveillance capitalism, manipulative design
- **Psychological failures** — Attention hijacking, addiction mechanics, burnout design
- **Systemic failures** — Platform consolidation, incentive misalignment, regulatory capture

## How This Site Is Built

Webshit practices what it preaches:

- Static HTML generated by [Jekyll](https://jekyllrb.com/) — no server-side processing
- Hosted on [GitHub Pages](https://pages.github.com/) — free and reliable
- Styled with [Pico CSS](https://picocss.com/) — 6KB, classless, semantic
- Analytics by [GoatCounter](https://www.goatcounter.com/) — no cookies, no tracking pixels
- No JavaScript frameworks. No newsletter signup modals. No cookie banners.
- Loads in under 1 second on 3G
